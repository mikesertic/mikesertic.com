about mike sertic

Mike Sertic has spent his career at the intersection of political education, organizational innovation, and the question that connects them both: how do people best govern themselves?

He got his start as part of the founding team of Students For Liberty, helping build what became one of the largest international networks of pro-liberty student organizations in the world — creating programs, content, and infrastructure that reached campuses across dozens of countries. That early experience in movement-building shaped a lasting conviction: that the right tools and frameworks can fundamentally change how people engage with political ideas.

From there, Mike moved to the Self-Management Institute at Morning Star Company — the world's largest tomato processor and one of the most radical experiments in non-hierarchical organization ever attempted at industrial scale. There, he studied how thousands of people coordinate complex operations through voluntary agreements rather than top-down authority. It was an education in what self-governance actually looks like when it's working — not as an abstract principle, but as a daily operational reality.

Today, Mike serves as President of the Advocates for Self-Government, a national nonprofit and the organization behind the World's Smallest Political Quiz. The Quiz has been taken by over 30 million people over its 40-year history, making it the most widely used political assessment tool ever created. Mike leads the organization's strategy, fundraising, operations, and program development — including Quiz in the Classroom, which brings nonpartisan political education into schools, and Operation Politically Homeless, a grassroots outreach program that has been used at thousands of public events nationwide.

He is also the creator of Political DNA, an independent political assessment platform he designed and built from scratch. Where most political quizzes flatten your worldview onto a single axis, Political DNA maps individuals across 32 distinct political archetypes — a more honest and more useful picture of where someone actually stands. It's the logical next step from the Quiz's legacy: same commitment to helping people understand themselves politically, rebuilt for a generation that expects more nuance and more depth.

The thread through all of it — from student organizing to self-managed factories to the world's most popular political quiz to a new kind of political identity tool — is a belief that people are capable of far more self-understanding and self-direction than most institutions give them credit for. Mike builds the tools that prove it.

He lives in the Sacramento area with his family.

Why the two-party framing of politics fails most Americans — and what a more honest map of political identity actually looks like.

Lessons from Morning Star and other non-hierarchical organizations on what happens when you take self-management seriously at scale.

Political Education That Works

What a decade-plus of movement-building has taught about converting political curiosity into lasting civic engagement.

Mike Sertic is President of the Advocates for Self-Government, the nonprofit organization behind the World's Smallest Political Quiz — the most widely taken political quiz in history, with over 30 million participants. He is also the creator of Political DNA, a political assessment platform that maps individuals across 32 archetypes beyond the traditional left-right spectrum. He lives in the Sacramento area.
